The Megason Lab uses innovative imaging techniques, combined with micropipettes aspirating air to hold onto single cells, to provide the first direct biophysical evidence for the differential adhesion hypothesis.

Symmetry Breaking in Gastruloids is Driven by Differential Adhesion

The Toettcher Lab combines gastruloids with a synthetic signaling reporter, which itself combines Cre lineage tracing with signaling reporters, to show that symmetry breaking in gastruloids is driven by differential adhesion downstream of developmental signaling pathways.

McNamara et al., Toettcher, Nat. Cel. Bio. (2024)
Science

The Logic of Monsters: Evidence for Internal Constraint in Development and Evolution

Alberch uses the existence of 'monsters'--phenotypes that are not adaptively favorable but occur reccurrently throughout history--among other lines of evidence (the vertebrate limb, transplant experiments, Hox genes, systems biology perspective) to argue for an internalist approach to development. He postulates that the parameter space of phenotypes is discontinuous due to inherent rules in developmental processes that limit possible phenotypes, arguing against the traditional neo-Darwinistic view taken by externalists, which roughly argues that mutation (noise) at the genetic level combined with natural selection (direction) act on a homogeneous phenotypic parameter space to drive the evolution of developmental forms, resulting in the discontinuities in the parameter space we observe in nature.

Alberch, 1989

Art

Pierre Soulages at the Montreal Museum of Fine Art

Ever since learning about Soulages' unique style and simple use of black and its properties, I've been searching for a museum with a Soulages piece for years now, and stumbled upon one by accident in Montreal. I was not disappointed. Painting, 130.2 x 162.5 cm, July 27, 1956 was impressive and foreboding, with the faint red in the background contrasting with the vertical black paintstrokes and making them almost 3D--almost as if they were advancing towards you for an unfriendly embrace.

Wislawa Szymborska's Nobel Prize Speech

A beautiful speech from a beautiful poet about what it means to be a poet in today's world, and the importance of being able to say "I don't know"--for poets and scientists alike. "This is why I value that little phrase "I don't know" so highly. It's small, but it flies on mighty wings. It expands our lives to include the spaces within us as well as those outer expanses in which our tiny Earth hangs suspended."
